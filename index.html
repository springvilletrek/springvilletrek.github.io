Physical Preparation

Trek involves walking several miles each day while pulling handcarts. Start preparing now by walking regularly and building endurance. Wear the shoes you plan to use on trek to break them in.

Spiritual Preparation

Study the 2026 youth theme — "Walk with Me" (Moses 6:34). Learn about pioneer history and the sacrifices early Latter-day Saints made. Come with a willing heart and a desire to grow closer to the Savior.

General Information

  • Pajamas
  • 1 or 2 bandanas (Stake provides)

  • Personal hygiene needs
  • 1 small flashlight with extra batteries
  • 1 washcloth and small hand towel
  • 1 travel-size package of wet towelettes
  • 1 small bottle of hand sanitizer
  • 1 small bottle of baby powder or Gold Bond

  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Insect repellent (mosquito, tick, and deer fly protection)
  • Chapstick (SPF 15+)

Clothing Guidelines

Three ways to get your pioneer clothing: thrift/modify, sew, or buy ready-made.

Thrift & Modify

This is the most economical approach and can be very successful! Look for natural fabrics (cotton, linen) in earth tones. Many modern items can be modified to look period-appropriate.

Tips for Finding & Modifying

  • Young Men: Look for cotton work shirts, suspenders, and dark cotton or canvas pants. Remove modern logos or labels.
  • Young Women: Look for long cotton skirts, simple blouses, and calico or gingham fabrics. Add an apron to make it more authentic.
  • Check DI, Goodwill, and other thrift stores regularly — inventory changes weekly
  • Look in the men's section for women's items — oversized men's shirts can become pioneer dresses
  • Avoid polyester, rayon, and synthetic fabrics — they don't breathe well
  • Bring items to the clothing workshop on March 15 for modification help

Sew from Patterns

If you enjoy sewing or want the most authentic look, making your own clothing from patterns is a great option. Attend the clothing workshop on March 15 for help with patterns and measurements.

Recommended Patterns

  • Young Men's Shirt: Simplicity 5441 or McCall's M7398
  • Young Men's Pants: Simplicity 7216 or Butterick B6690
  • Young Women's Dress: Simplicity 8397 or Butterick B4552
  • Apron: Simplicity 8192 or any simple apron pattern
  • Bonnet: Simplicity 8285 or search "pioneer bonnet pattern"

Fabric Recommendations

  • 100% cotton in earth tones (tan, brown, rust, green, blue)
  • Calico, gingham, or muslin for women's dresses
  • Canvas or heavy cotton for men's pants
  • Linen is authentic but expensive — save it for special touches
  • Avoid bright colors, modern prints, or anything with shine

Buy Ready-Made

If you prefer to buy ready-made clothing, several vendors specialize in pioneer trek attire. This is the quickest option but generally the most expensive. Order early to ensure availability and proper sizing.

Sizing Tips

  • Order clothing that is slightly larger — youth will grow between now and June
  • Loose-fitting clothing is more comfortable and historically accurate
  • Read vendor reviews about sizing — some run small or large
  • Allow 4–6 weeks for delivery, especially during trek season (May–July)

Clothing Requirements

What's Required

Young Men Must Have:

  • Long-sleeve cotton shirt (light color)
  • Long pants (dark color, no jeans)
  • Suspenders or belt
  • Wide-brimmed hat or cap
  • Bandana or neckerchief

Young Women Must Have:

  • Long dress or skirt & blouse (ankle length)
  • Apron (full or half)
  • Bonnet or pioneer-style hat
  • Modest undergarments (no sports bras with thick straps showing)
  • Long sleeves or layering piece for modesty

Important Guidelines

  • All clothing must be in earth tones (tan, brown, rust, green, blue, gray) — no bright colors
  • Natural fabrics only (cotton, linen) — synthetics don't breathe and will be uncomfortable
  • No modern logos, zippers showing, or obvious modern features
  • Clothing will be inspected before departure — bring only approved items
  • You'll wear this clothing for 5 days straight — make sure it's comfortable!
